# PowerBI
Excited to share insights from my latest Hospital Data Analysis Dashboard built in Power BI. This dashboard focuses on patient trends, departmental performance, and revenue analysis to support data-driven healthcare decisions.

 Key Insights:

 Department Performance

Neurology contributes the highest treatment revenue (~10M+), indicating high-cost specialized procedures.

General and Ortho departments also show strong revenue performance.

Cardiology has comparatively lower revenue, highlighting potential growth or efficiency improvement areas.

 Doctor-wise Revenue Analysis

Revenue contribution varies significantly among the 8 doctors.

Top-performing doctors generate over 5M+ in treatment cost.

This highlights specialization impact and performance concentration.

Patient Demographics

Gender distribution is balanced:

Male: 52.3%

Female: 47.7%

Indicates equal healthcare access and service utilization.

 Monthly Patient Trend

Peak patient count observed in January, April, and October.

Slight decline during mid-year months like July and September.

Seasonal patterns can support better workforce and resource planning.

Key KPIs

Average Treatment Cost: 38.10K

Total Doctors: 8

Strong revenue per doctor ratio indicates efficient hospital operations.

Length of Stay vs Treatment Cost

Positive correlation between longer hospital stay and higher treatment cost.

Departments with longer stays tend to generate higher revenue.

Opportunity to optimize operational efficiency without affecting patient care quality.
